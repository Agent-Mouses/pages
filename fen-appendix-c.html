3. Could Travel Nurses Inflate Our Results?

"What if the few years where travel nurses earned more made our penalty look bigger than it really is?"

1
Travel nurses are only 2.8% of all RNs (2022, the peak year)
2
In 2022 (single worst year), their taxable wage was +18.1% above average
3
A small group (+18%) in a large pool (2.8%) can only move the average by:

5. Table C3 — EB-3 Philippines: Consular (IVP) vs. In-US Adjustment (AOS)

High IVP% = direct overseas recruitment active. High AOS% = backlog clearing, few new arrivals.

Reading: When IVP (blue) dominates → nurses are being directly recruited from the Philippines with 3-year contracts → Group C grows → wage penalty is detectable. When AOS (gray) dominates → retrogression is blocking new entries → Group C shrinks → penalty becomes undetectable.
